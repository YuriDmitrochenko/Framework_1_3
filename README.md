Framework 1.3 is the fourth monograph in a series that began with Framework 1.0. Where the earlier works (1.0–1.2) laid down the general principles of cell-state architecture and examined oncogenesis and dormant cells as a phenomenon, the present monograph brings the molecular level together into a single picture: precisely which receptors, signaling cascades, and energetic circuits physically implement a cell's transitions between the active, dormant, differentiated, and senescent states — and how a failure within this architecture leads to oncogenesis.
The monograph did not grow from a single plan but from a sequence of questions, each one demanding the next step: from testing hypoxia and cell division — to the energetics of cell death — to transitions between states — to the formal, mathematical side of those transitions — and, finally, to cancer as a systemic failure of the entire architecture.
